Our Mission

Language is the vessel of culture, memory, and identity.

When a language disappears, so does an irreplaceable way of seeing the world. The Center for Low-Resource Languages and Cultures exists to reverse that tide — through rigorous research, community partnerships, and open technology.

We work hand-in-hand with indigenous communities, diaspora groups, and scholars to build tools and archives that keep endangered languages alive and thriving.

What We Do

Three pillars of lasting impact

01 —
📖

Documentation

Systematic field recording of oral traditions, grammars, lexicons, and endangered dialects — creating permanent digital archives accessible to future generations.

02 —
⚙️

Technology

Building open-source NLP tools, speech recognition datasets, and AI models for languages that big tech has left behind. Making language tech equitable and inclusive.

03 —
🤝

Community

Empowering native speaker communities as the true owners of their linguistic heritage — funding grassroots revival programs, schools, and cultural events worldwide.

Our Programs

Initiatives built for lasting change

From fieldwork fellowships to AI research grants, our programs support the full lifecycle of language preservation — from first contact to digital immortality.

Fieldwork Fellowships

Fund 6–18 month immersive documentation projects with endangered-language communities around the world.

Open Language Dataset Initiative

Collaboratively building the world's largest open corpus of low-resource language data for AI training.

Community Revival Grants

Direct funding to indigenous-led programs developing teaching materials, apps, and cultural media.

Language Tech Residency

A 3-month residency pairing engineers with linguists to build practical NLP tools for underserved communities.

Global Speaker Network

Connecting diaspora speakers worldwide to practice, document, and teach their heritage languages.

Youth Language Ambassadors

Training young people from language communities as ambassadors, educators, and digital content creators.
